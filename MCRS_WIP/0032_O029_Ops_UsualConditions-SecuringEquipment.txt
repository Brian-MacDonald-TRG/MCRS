					<specpara>
						<note>
							<trim.para>Try to secure safety chains to un-sprung weight such as an axle, A-Arm mount, etc. Securing equipment at front tow eyes or spring hangers allows the chassis to "bounce" on the suspension which momentarily slacks/shocks the safety chains during transport.</trim.para>
						</note>
						<para>Perform lift tow procedure as described in <xref wpid="O00013-9-2320-451"/>.</para>
					</specpara>
				</step1>
				<step1>
					<para>Position 1/2" chain at an un-sprung point (axle, lower a-arm, suspension mount, etc.) <callout assocfig="O00029-9-2320-451-F0001" label="1"/>.</para>
				</step1>

					<figure id="O00029-9-2320-451-F0001">
					<title>Safety Chain to Solid, Un-Sprung Mounting Point</title>
					<graphic boardno="G06232000198"></graphic>
					</figure>

				<step1>
					<para>Bring the safety chain to the outside of the booms and under the wheel stop <callout assocfig="O00029-9-2320-451-F0002" label="1"/>.</para>
				</step1>

					<figure id="O00029-9-2320-451-F0002">
					<title>Safety Chain Outside Booms and Under Wheel Stop</title>
					<graphic boardno="G06232000199"></graphic>
					</figure>

				<step1>
					<specpara>
						<note>
							<trim.para>An alternative method may be required.</trim.para>
						</note>
						<para>Route the chain through the D-Ring at the base of the booms and secure with a binder <callout assocfig="O00029-9-2320-451-F0003" label="1"/>.</para>
					</specpara>
				</step1>

					<figure id="O00029-9-2320-451-F0003">

					<graphic boardno="G06232000200"></graphic>
					</figure>

				<step1>
					<para>Perform Steps 2-4 on the other side of the disabled vehicle.</para>
				</step1>
			</proc>
			<proc>
				<title>ALTERNATIVE METHOD FOR SECURING VEHICLE</title>
				<step1>
					<para>Run the left and right chains up through the vertical tubes on the receivers.</para>
				</step1>
				<step1>
					<para>Join the chains in the center with one binder.</para>
				</step1>
				<step1>
					<para>Be sure the binder and chains are positioned over the 18K winch cable.</para>
				</step1>

					<figure id="O00029-9-2320-451-F0004">

					<specpara>
						<note>
							<trim.para>When securing loads on the TDRT, utilize 5/8" chains at the primary tie-down locations. Auxiliary tie-downs can use 1/2" chains as needed.</trim.para>
						</note>
						<para>Using 5/8" chains and binders, secure loads to the primary front.</para>
					</specpara>
				</step1>
				<step1>
