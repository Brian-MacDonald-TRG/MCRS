		</wpidinfo>
		<intro>
			<para0>
				<title>General</title>
				<para>The following information describes PMCS procedures on the operator level. The PMCS table has been provided to ensure the Modular Catastrophic Recovery System (MCRS) is in proper operating condition, and ready for use.</para>
				<subpara1>
					<title>Frequency of Performing PMCS</title>
					<para>PMCS will be performed before equipment is packed for use, during modification and repair after use, or at any time deemed necessary by the maintenance officer.</para>
				</subpara1>
				<subpara1>
					<title>PMCS Columnar Entries Table 1</title>
					<para>
						<emphasis emph="italic">Item Number.</emphasis>
						The item number column shall be used as a source of the item number required for the TM Number column on DA Form 2404/ DA 5988E (Equipment Inspection and Maintenance Worksheet), when recording the results of the PMCS.
					</para>
					<para>
						<emphasis emph="italic">Interval.</emphasis>
						This column identifies the required PMCS interval.
					</para>
					<para>
						<emphasis emph="italic">Item to be inspected.</emphasis >
						Contains the common name of the item to be inspected.
					</para>
					<para>
						<emphasis emph="italic">Procedures.</emphasis>
						Provides a brief description of the procedures by which the checks are to be performed.
					</para>
					<para>
						<emphasis emph="italic">Equipment Not Ready/Available If:</emphasis>
						Indicates faults which will prevent the equipment from performing its primary mission.
					</para>
				</subpara1>
				<subpara1>
					<title>Recording Defects</title>
					<para>
						All defects discovered during the inspection will be recorded using the applicable specifics in
						<extref docno="DA PAM 738-750" />
						,
						<extref docno="DA PAM 738-751"/>
						, and
						<extref docno="TB 43-0002-34"/>
						.
					</para>
				</subpara1>
				<subpara1>
					<title>Over Age Items</title>
					<para>
						During any inspection, or at any time that an item is found to be over age (i.e., shelf/service-life has expired as specified in
						<extref docno="TB 43-0002-34"/>
						), the item will be removed from service, condemned, and tagged, in accordance with
						<extref docno="DA PAM 738-751"/>
						.
					</para>
				</subpara1>
				<subpara1>
					<title>Conservation of Resources</title>
					<para>To conserve time and labor, unit/detachment commanders may designate, in writing, personnel to accomplish classification inspection of over age equipment and the classification of Beyond Economical Repair (BER).</para>
				</subpara1>
				<subpara1>
					<title>Inspection Function Requirement</title>
					<para>Normally, equipment maintenance personnel at a packing or repair section activity will perform an inspection. The inspection of initial receipt items will be performed.</para>
					<para>Should defect or damage be discovered at any point during the inspection, the inspection will be terminated and the applicable item will be processed and forwarded to repair activity. The repair activity, in turn, will conduct an inspection.</para>
					<para>Preventive Maintenance Checks and Services (PMCS) are performed to keep the MCRS in good operating condition and ready for its primary mission. The checks are used to find, correct, and report problems. PMCS is performed every day the MCRS is in operation, and is done according to the PMCS table provided. Pay attention to WARNING, CAUTION, and NOTE statements. A WARNING indicates that someone could be hurt or killed. A CAUTION indicates that equipment could be damaged. A NOTE may make your maintenance or repair task easier.</para>
					<para>Be sure to perform scheduled PMCS. Always perform PMCS in the same order so it becomes habit. With practice, you will quickly recognize problems with the equipment.</para>
					<para>Use DA Form 2404/ DA 5988E, Equipment Inspection and Maintenance Worksheet, to record any discovered faults. Do not record faults that you fix.</para>
				</subpara1>
				<subpara1>
					<title>PMCS PROCEDURES</title>
					<para>Table 1 lists inspections and care required to be performed by the operator to keep your equipment in good operating condition. It is arranged so that you can perform operational checks as you walk around the equipment.</para>
				</subpara1>
				<subpara1>
					<title>Explanation of Table 1 Columns</title>
					<para></para>
				</subpara1>
				<subpara1>
					<title>Item Number</title>
					<para>Indicates the reference number. When completing DA Form 2404 / DA 5899E, Equipment Inspection and Maintenance Worksheet, include the item number for the item to check/service indicating a fault. Item numbers appear in the order you must perform the checks/services listed.</para>
				</subpara1>
				<subpara1>
					<title>Interval</title>
					<para>
						Indicates when you must perform the procedure in the procedure column.
						<randlist bullet="yes">
							<item>Before - perform before equipment operation</item>
							<item>During - perform during equipment operation.</item>
							<item>After - perform after equipment has been operated</item>
							<item>Quarterly - perform every three months</item>
							<item>Semi-annually - perform every 6 months</item>
							<item>Annually - perform every 12 months</item>
							<item>Hours - perform at the noted hourly interval</item>
						</randlist>
					</para>
				</subpara1>
				<subpara1>
					<title>Item to Check/Service</title>
					<para>Indicates the item to be checked or serviced.</para>
				</subpara1>
				<subpara1>
					<title>Procedure</title>
					<para>Indicates the procedure you must perform on the item listed in Item to Check/Service column. You must perform the procedure at the time specified in the Interval column.</para>
				</subpara1>
				<subpara1>
					<title>Not Fully Mission Capable If:</title>
					<para>Indicates faults which will prevent your equipment from performing its primary mission. If you perform procedures listed in Procedure column which show faults listed in this column, do not operate the equipment. Follow standard procedures for maintaining the equipment or reporting equipment failure.</para>
				</subpara1>
				<subpara1>
					<title>Other special entries</title>
					<para>Observe all special information and notes that appear in Table 1.</para>
					<para>When a check/service procedure is required for both weekly and before intervals, it is not necessary to perform the procedure twice if the equipment is operated during the weekly period.</para>
				</subpara1>
				<subpara1>
					<title>Other special entries</title>
					<para>Observe all special information and notes that appear in Table 1.</para>
					<para>When a check/service procedure is required for both weekly and before intervals, it is not necessary to perform the procedure twice if the equipment is operated during the weekly period.</para>
				</subpara1>
				<subpara1>
					<title>Fluid Leakage</title>
					<para>It is necessary for you to know how fluid leakage affects the status of the MCRS. Following are types/classes of leakage you need to know to be able to determine the status of the MCRS. Learn these leakage definitions and remember - when in doubt, notify your supervisor. Consideration must be given to fluid capacity in the item/system being checked/inspected. When in doubt, notify your supervisor.</para>
					<para>
						When operating with Class I or II leaks, continue to check fluid levels as required in the PMCS. Class III leaks should be reported immediately to your supervisor.
						<randlist bullet="yes">
							<item>Class I. Seepage of fluid (as indicated by wetness or discoloration) not great enough to form drops.</item>
							<item>Class II. Leakage of fluid great enough to form drops but not enough to cause drops to drip from the item being checked/inspected.</item>
							<item>Class III. Leakage of fluid great enough to form drops that fall from item being checked/inspected.</item>
						</randlist>
					</para>
				</subpara1>
			</para0>
		</intro>
